About Me

My name is Isabelle Yiu. I am a full-stack software engineer who are passionate about using technology to make social impacts.

I worked as a local journalist and editor for a daily newspaper in the past 6 years, It was my job to help the Chinese immigrant community by writing about social and political injustices in San Francisco.

After taking a udemy course on programming and building some small projects as a side hobby. I realized I can be more than just a voice for the issues that I had been writing about. I can use technology to bring about change!

As part of my Software Engineering Apprenticeship program at Techtonica, a non-profit coding program that provide over 1000 hours of full-stack JavaScript web development learning with acceptance rate less than 9%, I designed and created a project called Housing Alert that aims to close the digital divide by making the process of finding affordable housing accessible for people who are not on the internet.

As of July 2019, I am working as a Software Developer Intern with Indeed.com to help over 200 millions people to get jobs! Learn more about my work with the Data Science Platform team on my LinkedIn.

Projects

Housing Alert

Designed and developed Housing Alert from past experience working as a local reporter in San Francisco.

Housing alert is a web app and sms-based free service that aims to close the digital divide by making the process of finding and applying to affordable housing accessible for people who don’t have internet service or don’t know how to use the internet.

Technologies used:

JavaScript | React | Node | Express | PostgreSQL | Sequelize | React Bootstrap | Twilio | Passport.js | OAuth authentication | Heroku
